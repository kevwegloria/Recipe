Indomie Noodle Soup Recipe

ORIGIN

Instant noodles were first introduced and marketed in 1958 in Japan. Due to its practicality and delicious taste, instant noodles were well received and liked by the Japanese. Soon after, its popularity spread throughout the world.Indomie instant noodles brand was first launched in 1972 with Indomie Chicken flavour. In 1982, Indomie launched Mi Goreng, the first dry noodles variant (consumed without broth), inspired by the traditional Indonesian fried noodles dish.

INGREDIENTS

Indomie 2 packets
Boiled eggs 2
Vegetable stock 1 cup
Carrot 1
Peas 1 cup
Green pepper 1
Red onion 1/2
Spring onion 1
Clove garlic 1
Crushed ginger 1 tsp
Light soy sauce 1 tbsp

INSTRUCTIONS

1. I used pre-made vegetable stock but you can make your own by boiling some vegetables _carrots, onion, garlic, ginger, leeks, celery with lots of water. If not you can still use water with stock cubes.

2. Start by cutting carrots and green peppers into julienne/thin strips

3. Cut up spring onions and red onion thinly

4. In a skillet under medium heat, cook garlic and ginger

5. Add soy sauce and vegetable stock and bring to a boil.

6. Cook noodles as per the instructions.

7. Place noodles into two bowls.

8. Add the broth, then add the carrots, green peppers, onion, spring onions and the boiled eggs.

9. Serve with bumbu sauce and enjoy.
